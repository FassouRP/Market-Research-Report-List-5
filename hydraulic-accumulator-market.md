<p><p>Hydraulic accumulators are devices used to store hydraulic energy and maintain pressure in a hydraulic system. There are four main types of hydraulic accumulators based on their construction and operation: bladder, piston, diaphragm, and other. Bladder accumulators use a flexible bladder to separate the gas and hydraulic fluid, while piston accumulators use a piston to separate the two. Diaphragm accumulators use a flexible diaphragm to achieve the same result. Other types of accumulators may have unique designs or features tailored to specific applications.</p></p>
