<p><p>Immunoglobulins, also known as antibodies, play a crucial role in our immune system. There are five main types of immunoglobulins: IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, and IgD. Each type has specific functions in fighting off infections and pathogens. IgG is the most abundant and versatile antibody, providing long-term immunity. IgA is found in mucosal tissues and helps prevent infections at mucosal surfaces. IgM is the first antibody produced in response to an infection. IgE is involved in allergic reactions. IgD's function is still not well understood.</p></p>
