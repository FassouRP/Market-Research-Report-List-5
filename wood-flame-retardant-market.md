<p><p>The wood flame retardant market can be segmented into inorganic type and organic type. Inorganic flame retardants contain minerals such as alumina trihydrate and magnesium hydroxide, which act as heat-absorbing barriers. Organic flame retardants are composed of carbon-based compounds that release water or carbon dioxide when exposed to heat, creating a protective layer. Inorganic types are preferred for their non-toxic nature and durability, while organic types are valued for their versatility and effectiveness in reducing smoke emissions.</p></p>
