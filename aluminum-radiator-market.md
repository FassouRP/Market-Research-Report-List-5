<p><p>Aluminum radiators can be classified into two main types based on the materials used in their construction. The first type is made of high die casting aluminum, which involves forming the radiator using high-pressure injection of molten aluminum into a mold. The second type is made through tensile welding of aluminum alloy, where different aluminum alloys are welded together to create the radiator. Both types offer various benefits such as lightweight construction, high heat transfer efficiency, and durability in automotive applications.</p></p>
