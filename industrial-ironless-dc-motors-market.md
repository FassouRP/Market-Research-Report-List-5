<p><p>Industrial Ironless DC Motors are classified into two main types: Brushless Motors and Brushed Motors. Brushless Motors use electronic commutation instead of brushes, resulting in higher efficiency, lower maintenance, and smoother operation. These motors are ideal for applications requiring high precision and reliability. On the other hand, Brushed Motors use brushes to transfer electrical power to the rotor, making them simpler and more cost-effective. However, they tend to have shorter lifespan and higher maintenance requirements compared to Brushless Motors.</p></p>
