<p><p>Transcatheter Mitral Valve Repair involves treating a patient's mitral valve without the need for open-heart surgery. This procedure can be done by deploying a device through a catheter, repairing the valve and improving its function. On the other hand, Transcatheter Mitral Valve Replacement is a procedure where a new valve is inserted through a catheter to replace a malfunctioning mitral valve. These minimally invasive approaches offer less pain, shorter recovery times, and lower risks compared to traditional open-heart surgery.</p></p>
