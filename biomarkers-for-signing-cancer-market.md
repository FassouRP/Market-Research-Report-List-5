<p><p>Biomarkers for cancer detection can be classified into various types such as genetic biomarkers, protein biomarkers, and glyco-biomarkers. Genetic biomarkers involve the analysis of an individual's genetic makeup to identify specific mutations associated with cancer. Protein biomarkers involve the detection of specific proteins in the body that are indicative of cancer. Glyco-biomarkers focus on the analysis of sugar molecules attached to proteins, which can provide valuable information about cancer development and progression. Each type of biomarker plays a crucial role in the diagnosis and treatment of cancer.</p></p>
