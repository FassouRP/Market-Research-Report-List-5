<p><p>Electron Microscopy (SEM) and Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) are two main types of electron microscopes used in scientific research and industry. SEM uses a focused beam of electrons to create a detailed surface image of a sample, while TEM directs electrons through a thin sample to produce a high-resolution image of its internal structure. Both techniques offer unparalleled magnification and resolution, making them indispensable tools in fields such as materials science, biology, and nanotechnology. Their widespread applications drive the growth of the electron microscope market.</p></p>
