<p><ul><li>MAP (Monoammonium Phosphate)</li><li>DAP (Diammonium Phosphate)</li><li>Rock Phosphate</li><li>Super Phosphate</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Phosphate fertilizers can be divided into various types including MAP (Monoammonium Phosphate), DAP (Diammonium Phosphate), Rock Phosphate, and Super Phosphate. MAP and DAP are water-soluble fertilizers containing nitrogen and phosphorous. Rock phosphate is a natural mineral source of phosphorous, while Super Phosphate is a synthetic fertilizer produced by treating rock phosphate with sulfuric acid. Each type of phosphate fertilizer has its own specific benefits and uses in agriculture, catering to different nutrient requirements of crops.</p></p>
