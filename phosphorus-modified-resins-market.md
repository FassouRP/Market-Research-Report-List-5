<p><p>Phosphorus-modified resins refer to materials that have been enhanced with phosphorus compounds for improved fire resistance and thermal stability. Two common types in the market are DOPO Modified Novolac Epoxy (DOPO-NE) and Vinyl Phosphates. DOPO-NE resins are epoxy resins modified with 9,10-dihydro-9-oxa-10-phosphaphenanthrene-10-oxide (DOPO) for enhanced flame retardancy. Vinyl Phosphates are resins that incorporate phosphorus-containing vinyl monomers to improve fire resistance properties. Both types play a crucial role in various industries where fire safety is a priority.</p></p>
