<p><p>Color filters for displays come in two main types: RGB primary color filters and CMYG complementary color filters. RGB filters use red, green, and blue subpixels to create a wide range of colors by combining these primary colors. On the other hand, CMYG filters use cyan, magenta, yellow, and green subpixels to achieve a similar color spectrum by mixing complementary colors. Both types of filters play a crucial role in determining the color accuracy and vibrancy of displays in various electronic devices.</p></p>
