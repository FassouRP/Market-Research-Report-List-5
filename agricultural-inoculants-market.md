<p><p>Agricultural inoculants can be categorized into two main types: seed inoculants and soil inoculants. Seed inoculants are applied directly to seeds before planting to introduce beneficial microorganisms, such as nitrogen-fixing bacteria, to the plant. Soil inoculants are applied directly to the soil to enhance its quality by adding beneficial microorganisms, such as mycorrhizae, which promote plant growth. Both types of inoculants play a crucial role in improving crop yield and overall plant health in agriculture.</p></p>
