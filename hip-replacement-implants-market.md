Ceramic implants are resistant to wear and provide smooth movement but are more prone to fracture. Polyethylene implants are lightweight and have low wear rates but may wear down over time. Each type has its own benefits and drawbacks, and the choice of implant material depends on the patient's specific needs and preferences.</p></p>
