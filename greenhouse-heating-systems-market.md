<p><p>Greenhouse heating systems typically fall into four main types: air heaters, fuel heaters, cable heaters, and other miscellaneous systems. Air heaters use fans to distribute heated air throughout the greenhouse, while fuel heaters burn a fuel source such as gas or oil to generate heat. Cable heaters consist of electric heating cables that are installed in the soil to warm the plant roots. Other systems may include heat pumps, geothermal systems, or solar heating. Each type of system has its own advantages and may be suitable for different greenhouse setups.</p></p>
